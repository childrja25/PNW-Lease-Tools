## Section Details

### 1. Quarter at a Glance
**Position:** Top of page - visual impact  
**Purpose:** Instant value. Reader knows market pulse in 5 seconds.

**Include:**
- Total investment sales volume (vs prior quarter, YoY)
- Average cap rate by product type
- Leasing velocity or absorption
- One standout stat that surprises

**Format:** Big numbers, arrows showing direction, minimal text

---

### 2. Top Sales (2-3 deals max)
**Purpose:** Concrete comps your clients actually need

**Include for each deal:**
- Property name & address
- Sale price
- Price per SF
- Cap rate (if available)
- Buyer/seller (if public)
- One sentence on why it matters

**Selection criteria:** Largest deals OR deals that signal market direction

---

### 3. Notable Leasing (2-3 deals max)
**Purpose:** Show tenant demand and rental rate trends

**Include for each deal:**
- Tenant name
- Property/submarket
- Square footage
- Lease term
- Rental rate (if available)

**Focus on:** New-to-market tenants, expansions, or deals in emerging submarkets

---

### 4. Financing & Capital Markets (2-3 bullets)
**Purpose:** What clients can't easily find elsewhere

**Include:**
- Interest rate movements (Treasuries, spreads)
- Lender appetite by product type
- Notable financings you're aware of
- CMBS/agency trends affecting the region

> **This is your differentiator** - local intel on what's actually getting financed

---

### 5. What We're Watching
**Purpose:** This is the call-generation section

Write 2-3 sentences about:
- An emerging trend or concern
- A question you're asking yourself
- An invitation for the reader to share their view

**Example:**
> "We're seeing increased interest from out-of-state industrial buyers, but local owners remain hesitant to sell below 2022 pricing. How long does this standoff last? We'd love to hear what you're seeing."

**End with clear CTA:** "Hit reply if you'd like to discuss" or "Call me at..."

- "Cap rates for suburban retail tightened 25bps this quarter. Anomaly or trend? We're curious what you think."

## Design Tips

1. **Use your brand colors** - consistency builds recognition
2. **White space > wall of text** - scannable beats comprehensive
3. **Bold the numbers** - eyes go to figures first
4. **One font, two sizes** - headers + body, that's it
5. **Mobile-friendly** - 50%+ will read on phone

- **Open rate:** Target 35%+ (CRE industry avg is ~25%)
- **Click rate:** Target 5%+
- **Replies:** The real goal - aim for 2-3 per send
- **Meetings booked:** Track deals that started from newsletter
